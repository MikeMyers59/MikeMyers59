Electric Boat Ship Control Department

 

Ship Control and Monitoring Systems Engineering Department 448

The Ship Control and Monitoring Systems Engineering Department 448 develops in-house electronic systems and integrates vendor/government supplied equipment to support the VA Ship Control System (SCS), VA/OR Monitoring Systems, and the VA/OR Portable Ship Control Systems (PSCS).

The Virginia Class Ship Control System is a major technological improvement over the ship control systems of previous submarine classes. A fly-by-wire (FBW) system controls the previous hydraulic mechanical-based system which controls the ship's steering and diving performance. It provides significantly increased capabilities, exceptional reliability via software fault tolerance, and reduced manning via automation. The Portable Ship Control System leverages commercial electronics and ruggedizes the applications to support submarine bridge operations in very challenging operational and environmental conditions. To support fleet training, D448 developed ship control trainers based on electromechanical actuator (EMA) based motion platforms replacing hydraulic systems for all submarine classes. D448 develops and maintains the electronics and software for the Multi-Class, Seawolf, and Virginia Class Operational and Maintenance Trainers in Groton, Norfolk, Kings Bay, Bangor, San Diego, Hawaii and Guam.

The Monitoring Group is responsible for Total Ship Monitoring Systems (TSMS) on the OR and VA submarines. This group provides real time data acquisition and processing to assess the stealth of the submarine and provides the capabilities to identify and localize noise sources. The group specializes on the telemetry electronics and sensors needed to support the GFE monitoring systems. The group also is responsible for coordinating and integrating the TSMS GFE and CFE components throughout the submarine. In addition this group supports Internal Research and Development for the division's wireless network development for sensors and supports other EB groups implementing critical acoustic electronic systems for OR and VA.

D448 is the EB's technical authority for the FBW Special Emphasis program to ensure ship safety for the ship control electronic and software based system. This includes the FBW development processes, work controls, audits, and testing to ensure ship safety. Lastly, D448 supports EB and NNS construction activities and provides in-service engineering support to all delivered VA Class submarines to support troubleshooting, testing, maintenance and operational problems.
